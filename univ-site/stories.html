Stories

Pre-Class Reading

I arrived in Provo a week before school started, and right after I settled in, I got an email from the American Heritage professors. It was one of the classes I was taking, and they informed me that before the first day of class, there were four long readings I needed to complete, including a 26-page syllabus. That set a precedent for what the workload of college would be like. That got me really worried, and I started to stress. Eventually, I realized that not all assignments would be so extensive. From this experience, I learned that I need to remain optimistic and not let one experience ruin my perception of how things will be. This has enlarged my intellect.

First Day of Class

Within the first few moments of my first real class at BYU, I knew that I was in an environment where the Holy Spirit takes the lead in teaching. I was in my computer science class, and my professor shared a spiritual thought and then asked someone to say a prayer. I thought that was so cool. Throughout the week, I noticed that several of my professors did the same thing. For one, this helped me feel the Spirit, but more importantly, it helped me feel comfortable. I just returned from my mission, and being removed from that constantly-spiritually environment was a little hard for me. At this moment, I felt like I was at home, where I was supposed to be. That is why I know that BYU will help strengthen me spiritually.

Finding a Job

After a couple weeks of school, I started feeling like I needed to find work. Many of the people around me were working while doing school, and I decided I wanted to work as well. I started the job hunt, and at first it didn't really go anywhere. I was really putting a lot of effort into it, but none of the jobs that appealed to me were reaching out. I didn't want to do unskilled labor, and maybe it sounds prideful, but I knew I had experience that would qualify for lots of jobs, specifically computer science related jobs. After a lot of searching, I applied to a website development job, and I finally got an interview. The whole interview process went well, and I ended up getting the job. It's been a challenge for me to work and do school, but it's helped me learn to be productive with my time and balance multiple things.
